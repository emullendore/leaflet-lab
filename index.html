Sulfur Dioxide Levels in the Top 15 Most Polluted Cities (1990-2014)

Sulfur dioxide is a reactive gas emitted by power plants from the process of fossil fuel combustion (coal-fire). Standards for SO2 were first set by the EPA in 1971. Respiratory effects can arise from prolonged exposure to SO2. The substance is also a culprit of acid rain. In 1996, under the authority of the Clean Power Plan, the National Ambient Air Quality Standards were established. Standards set in 1971 were revised in 2010. The new 1-hour standard is 75 parts per billion. Symbols outlined in orange represent cities above this standard.

Cities on this map are the most polluted by year-round particle pollution, as determined by the American Lung Association's 2015 State of the Air. Sulfur dioxide is one component of particular pollution.

Coal-fired power plants show a strong correlation with SO2 pollution. These are coal-fired power plants with output over 2,000 MW.

It is important to acknowledge the progress made in air pollution reduction in the past decades, but to also look to the future to continually improve air quality in America.
