Clearing the Air - SO2 Pollution

Sulfur dioxide is a reactive gas emitted by power plants from the process of fossil fuel combustion (coal-fire). Standards for SO2 were first set by the EPA in 1971. Respiratory effects can arise from prolonged exposure to SO2. The substance is also a culprit of acid rain. In 1996, under the authority of the Clean Power Plan, the National Ambient Air Quality Standards were established. Standards set in 1971 were revised in 2010. The new 1-hour standard is 75 parts per billion. The symbols outlined in orange represent cities above this standard.

Cities on this map are the most polluted by year-round particle pollution, as determined by the American Lung Association's 2015 State of the Air. Sulfur dioxide is one component of particular pollution.

The coal-fired power plants with output over 2,000 MW are displayed. Coal-fired power plants show a strong correlation with SO2 pollution.
